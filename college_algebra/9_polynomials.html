9.1 Definitions

Let $a_0$, $a_1$, $a_2$, $\dots$, $a_{n-1}$, $a_n$ be real numbers and $n$ a natural number. The expression \[a_nx^n+a_{n-1}x^{n-1}+\cdots+a_2x^2+a_1x+a_0\] is called a polynomial in $x$ with real coefficients. Assuming $a_n\ne0$, this polynomial is said to be of degree $n$.

The numbers $a_0$, $a_1$, $a_2$, etc. are called coefficients and the expressions $a_0$, $a_1x$, $a_2x^2$, etc. are called terms. The expression $a_nx^n$ is called the leading term and $a_n$ the leading coefficient. The term $a_0$ is called the constant term.

For example, \[\frac 52x^2-\sqrt2 x+19.3\] is a polynomial in $x$ with real coefficients. It is of degree $2$, with leading term $\frac52 x^2$, leading coefficient $\frac52$, and constant term $19.3$.

9.2 Adding Polynomials

If \[p(x)=x^2-3x-11\] and \[q(x)=-2x^2+3x+13,\] then adding $q(x)$ to $p(x)$ results in the new polynomial \begin{align} p(x)+q(x)&=(x^2-3x-11)+(-2x^2+3x+13)\\ &=x^2-2x^2-3x+3x-11+13\\ &=-x^2+2. \end{align}

9.3 Subtracting Polynomials

If \[p(x)=x^2-3x-11\] and \[q(x)=-2x^2+3x+13,\] then subtracting $q(x)$ from $p(x)$ results in the new polynomial \begin{align} p(x)-q(x)&=(x^2-3x-11)-(-2x^2+3x+13)\\ &=(x^2-3x-11)+(2x^2-3x-13)\\ &=x^2+2x^2-3x-3x-11-13\\ &=3x^2-6x-24. \end{align}

9.4 Multiplying Polynomials

Recall:

  1. The distributive property of multiplication over addition in the real numbers: \[a(b+c)=ab+ac.\]
  2. The property of exponents: \[x^m\cdot x^n=x^{m+n}.\]

If \[p(x)=11x^5\text{ and }q(x)=5x-3,\] then multiplying $p(x)$ by $q(x)$ results in the new polynomial \begin{align} p(x)\cdot q(x)&=11x^5\cdot(5x-3)\\ &=11x^5\cdot 5x+11x^5\cdot(-3)\\ &=55x^6-33x^5. \end{align}

If \[p(x)=2x^2-9\text{ and }q(x)=5x^3+x+1,\] then multiplying $p(x)$ by $q(x)$ results in the new polynomial \begin{align} p(x)\cdot q(x)&=(2x^2-9)(5x^3+x+1)\\ &=(2x^2-9)\cdot5x^3+(2x^2-9)\cdot x+(2x^2-9)\cdot1\\ &=2x^2\cdot5x^3-9\cdot5x^3+2x^2\cdot x-9\cdot x+2x^2-9\\ &=10x^5-45x^3+2x^3-9x+2x^2-9\\ &=10x^5-43x^3+2x^2-9x-9. \end{align}
