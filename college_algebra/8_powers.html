Whole Number Powers

Definitions and Notation If $a$ is a real number and $n$ is a whole number, then the product \[\underbrace{a\cdot a\cdot \cdots\cdot a}_{n\text{ factors}}\] is called the $n^{\text{th}}$ power of $a$. For convenience, we can use the notation \[a^n:=\underbrace{a\cdot a\cdot\cdots\cdot a}_{n\text{ factors}}.\] In this notation, $a$ is called the base and $n$ the exponent. The notation can be read $a$ raised to the power of $n$ or $a$ (raised) to the $n^{\text{th}}$ (power).
Properties of (Whole Number) Powers For any real numbers $a$ and $b$ and any whole numbers $n$ and $m$:
  1. $\displaystyle a^n\cdot a^m=a^{n+m}$
  2. $(a\cdot b)^n=a^n\cdot b^n$
  3. $\displaystyle\left(\frac ab\right)^n=\frac{a^n}{b^n}$
  4. $(a^n)^m=a^{n\cdot m}$
Proof of 1. Indeed, \begin{align} a^n\cdot a^m&=\underbrace{a\cdot a\cdot\cdots\cdot a}_{n\text{ factors}}\cdot\underbrace{a\cdot a\cdot\cdots\cdot a}_{m\text{ factors}}\\ &=\underbrace{a\cdot a\cdot\cdots\cdot a}_{n+m\text{ factors}}\\ &=a^{m+n}. \end{align}
Show Proof
Proof of 2. Indeed, \begin{align} (a\cdot b)^n&=\underbrace{(a\cdot b)\cdot (a\cdot b)\cdot\cdots\cdot (a\cdot b)}_{n\text{ factors}}\\ &=\underbrace{a\cdot a\cdot\cdots\cdot a}_{n\text{ factors}}\cdot\underbrace{b\cdot b\cdot\cdots\cdot b}_{n\text{ factors}}\\ &=a^n\cdot b^n. \end{align} Show Proof
Proof of 3. Indeed, \begin{align} \left(\frac ab\right)^n&=\underbrace{\left(\frac ab\right)\cdot\left(\frac ab\right)\cdot\cdots\cdot \left(\frac ab\right)}_{n\text{ factors}}\\ &=\frac{\overbrace{a\cdot a\cdot\cdots\cdot a}^{n\text{ factors}}}{\underbrace{b\cdot b\cdot\cdots\cdot b}_{n\text{ factors}}}\\&=\frac{a^n}{b^n}. \end{align} Show Proof
Proof of 4. Indeed, \begin{align} (a^n)^m&=\left(\underbrace{a\cdot a\cdot\cdots\cdot a}_{n\text{ factors}}\right)^m\\ &=\underbrace{\left(\underbrace{a\cdot a\cdot\cdots\cdot a}_{n\text{ factors}}\right)\cdot \left(\underbrace{a\cdot a\cdot\cdots\cdot a}_{n\text{ factors}}\right)\cdot\cdots\cdot \left(\underbrace{a\cdot a\cdot\cdots\cdot a}_{n\text{ factors}}\right)}_{m\text{ factors}}\\ &=a^{n\cdot m}. \end{align}

Show Proof

Integer Powers

Definitions and Notation

If $a$ is a nonzero real number, then \[a^{-1}:=\frac1a\] and \[a^0:=1.\] If $n$ is a whole number, then \[a^{-n}:=\frac1{a^n}.\]

Properties of (Integer) Powers

For any real numbers $a$ and $b$ and any integers $n$ and $m$: \[a^n\cdot a^m=a^{n+m}\] \[(a\cdot b)^n=a^n\cdot b^n\] \[\left(\frac ab\right)^n=\frac{a^n}{b^n}\text{ when }b\ne0\] \[(a^n)^m=a^{n\cdot m}\] \[\frac{a^n}{a^m}=a^{n-m}\]

We have proven the first for properties for positive integers, so it remains to prove them for when one or both of $n$ and $m$ is $0$ and for when one or both of $n$ and $m$ is a negative integer.

Indeed, if $n=0$ and $m$ is any integer, then \[a^n\cdot a^m=a^0\cdot a^m=1\cdot a^m=a^m=a^{0+m},\] \[(a\cdot b)^n=(a\cdot b)^0=1=1\cdot 1=a^0\cdot b^0,\] \[\left(\frac ab\right)^n=\left(\frac ab\right)^0=1=\frac11=\frac{a^0}{b^0},\] and \[(a^n)^m=(a^0)^m=1^m=1=a^0=a^{0\cdot m}.\] If $m=0$ and $n$ is any integer, the first property clearly holds, and \[(a^n)^m=(a^n)^0=1=a^0=a^{n\cdot0}.\]

Rational Powers

Definitions and Notation

If $a$ is a nonzero real number and..., then $r=a^{1/n}$ is the real number satisfying $r^n=a$.

Properties of (Rational) Powers

For any real numbers $a$ and $b$ and any rational numbers $n$ and $m$: \[a^n\cdot a^m=a^{n+m}\] \[(a\cdot b)^n=a^n\cdot b^n\] \[\left(\frac ab\right)^n=\frac{a^n}{b^n}\text{ when }b\ne0\] \[(a^n)^m=a^{n\cdot m}\] \[\frac{a^n}{a^m}=a^{n-m}\]
