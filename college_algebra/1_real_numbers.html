Distributive Property What is The Distributive Property?

The Distributive Property (of multiplication of real numbers over addition of real numbers) is the property that any three real numbers $x$, $y$, and $z$ satisfy: $x(y+z)=xy+xz$ The Distributive Property is also known as left distributivity.

We can prove right distributivity (of multiplication of real numbers over addition of real numbers), the property that any three real numbers $x$, $y$, and $z$ satisfy:$(x+y)z=xz+yz$

Indeed, by commutativity of multiplication of real numbers, left distributivity, and commutativity of addition of real numbers, $(x+y)z=z(x+y)=zx+zy=xz+yz$. $\qed$ Applying Distributivity

Let's carefully apply distributivity to simplify some real expressions.

Let $a\in\R$. Simplify $3(a+4)+8$. See, $3(a+4)+8=3a+3\cdot4+8=3a+20$.

We'll be especially careful around minus signs.

Let $a,b\in\R$. Expand $5 (a-b)-1$. Writing $5(a-b)=5a+5-b$ is a common mistake which causes serious problems down the road. When we distribute the $5$, it should be multiplied to each the $a$ and $-b$. But, when we write $5-b$, we are expressing subtraction, not multiplication. We remedy the situation using parentheses. See, $5(a-b)-1=5a+5(-b)-1=5a-5b-1$.

Let's use right distributivity.

Let $a$ be a real number. Simplify $5+(a+8)3$. See, $5+(a+8)3=5+a3+8\cdot3=3a+5+24=3a+29$.

In the above example, we used commutativity of multiplication of real numbers to rewrite $a3$ as $3a$ and commutativity of addition of real numbers to reorganize the sum $5+3a$ as $3a+5$. The following is another example of using commutativity.

Let $a$ be a real number. Expand $4(a+5)3$. See, $4(a+5)3=4\cdot3(a+5)=12(a+5)=12a+12\cdot5=12a+60$. Alternatively, we could have started with distributivity, but we must be careful.
Writing $4(a+5)3=4a+4\cdot5\cdot3$ is a common mistake which causes serious problems down the road. The $3$ was to be multiplied to both terms in the sum $a+5$, but here it is only multiplying one of the terms in the sum $4a+4\cdot5$. We remedy the situation using parentheses. See, $4(a+5)3=(4a+4\cdot5)3=4a3+20\cdot3=4\cdot3a+60=12a+60$.

We increase the level of sophistication.

Let $a$ and $b$ be real numbers. Simplify $(3+a)(b+4)-3$. See, \begin{align}(3+a)(b+4)-3&=(3+a)b+(3+a)4-3\\ &=3b+ab+3\cdot4+a4-3\\ &=4a+3b+ab+12-3\\ &=4a+3b+ab+9\end{align}

Again.

Let $a$ and $b$ be real numbers. Simplify $3(a+2)(b-9)+11$. See, \begin{align}3(a+2)(b-9)+11&=3((a+2)b+(a+2)(-9))+11\\ &=3(ab+2b+a(-9)+2(-9))+11\\ &=3ab+3\cdot2b+3(-9a)+3(-18)+11\\ &=3ab+6b-27a-54+11\\ &=-27a+6b+3ab-43\end{align}
Negatives Definition

The negative of a real number $x$ is the number $-x$ such that $x+-x=0$.

Properties

Let $x$ and $y$ be real numbers.

$-(-x)=x$ $-x=-1\cdot x$ $-(x+y)=-x+-y$ $-x\cdot y=x\cdot-y=-(x\cdot y)$ $-x\cdot -y=x\cdot y$ Let $y\ne0$. $\frac{-x}{-y}=\frac xy$ $\frac{-x}y=\frac x{-y}=-\frac xy$ Examples

Using the properties above, we will simplify negatives.

Let $a$ be a real number. Simplify $2- (5+a)$. See, $2-(5+a)=2-5-a=-3-a$.

We increase the level of sophistication.

Let $u$ and $v$ be real numbers. Simplify $u- (3-v)-4$. See, $u-(3-v)-4=u-3-(-v)-4=u-3+v-4=u+v-3-4=u+v-7$.
Fractions Definitions

Let $x$ and $y$ are real numbers with $y\ne0$. It is convenient to use the notation $\frac xy:=xy^{-1}$. The object on the left is called a fraction. In this notation, $x$ is called the numerator of the fraction and $y$ the denominator of the fraction.

 Properties of Fractions Let $a$, $b$, $c$, and $d$ be real numbers with $b,d\ne0$. $x=\frac x1$ $\frac ab+\frac cd=\frac {ad+bc}{bd}$ $\frac ab-\frac cd=\frac {ad-bc}{bd}$ $\frac ab\cdot\frac cd=\frac {a\cdot c}{b\cdot d}$ $\frac ab=\frac {a\cdot d}{b\cdot d}$ If $c\ne0$, then $\left({\frac ab}\right)/\left({\frac cd}\right)=\frac{ad}{bc}$ Examples
