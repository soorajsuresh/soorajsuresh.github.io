Logarithmic Functions

Definition

If $x$ is a positive real number and $b$ is a positive real number different from $1$, then $\log_b(x)$ is the unique real number that satisfies $b^{\log_b(x)}=x$.

\[\log_b(x)=y\] means \[x=b^y\]

Properties

If $x$, $y$, and $b$ are positive numbers, and $b\ne1$, then \[\log_b(x)+\log_b(y)=\log_b(xy)\] and \[\log_b(x)-\log_b(y)=\log_b\left(\frac xy\right).\] If $p$ is a real number, then \[\log_b(x^p)=p\log_b(x).\]

For example, \begin{align} \log_3\left(\sqrt[5]{\frac{x^{11}}{81y^8}}\right)&=\frac15\log_3\left(\frac{x^{11}}{243y^8}\right)\\ &=\frac15\left(\log_3(x^{11})-\log_3(243y^8)\right)\\ &=\frac15\left(11\log_3(x)-\left(\log_3(243)+\log_3(y^8)\right)\right)\\ &=\frac15\left(11\log_3(x)-\left(4+8\log_3(y)\right)\right)\\ &=\frac15\left(11\log_3(x)-4-8\log_3(y)\right). \end{align}
