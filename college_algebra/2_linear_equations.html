2 Linear Equations

2.1 Linear Equations In One Variable

2.1.1 Definitions Let $a$ and $b$ be real numbers and $x$ a real variable. Any equation that can be written as \[ax+b=0\] is called a linear equation in one variable, assuming $a\ne0$. We solve a linear equation by using properties E1—E4 of real numbers to isolate the variable $x$.

2.1.2 Basic Examples

We will look at 7 basic examples.

Example Solve $x+3=17$. By subtracting $3$ from both sides of the equation, $x=14$.

The following are a couple of similar examples, each of which can be solved simply by subtracting (or adding) a real number.

Example Solve $x+\frac32=5$. By subtracting $\frac32$ from both sides of the equation, $x=\frac72$.
Example Solve $x-1000=350$. By adding $1000$ to both sides of the equation, $x=1350$.

Next, a slightly different example.

Example Solve $3x=6$. By dividing both sides of the equation by $3$, $x=2$.

The following are a couple of similar examples, each of which can be solved simply by dividing (or multiplying) by a real number.

Example Solve $3x=6$. By dividing both sides of the equation by $3$, $x=2$.
Example Solve $3x=6$. By dividing both sides of the equation by $3$, $x=2$.

The following example is an important special case similar to the previous example. We will use the following fact: $-x=-1\cdot x$, so $-1$$\cdot-x=$ $-1$ $\cdot-1\cdot x=1\cdot x=x$.

Example Solve $-x=54$. By multiplying both sides of the equation by $-1$, $x=-54$.

2.1.3 Sophisticated Examples

Next, we increase the level of sophistication by requiring both subtraction and division.

Example Solve $9x-2=-4$. By adding $2$ to both sides of the equation, $9x=-2$. By dividing both sides of the equation by $9$, $x=-\frac29$.

The following are a couple of similar examples, each of which can be solved simply by first subtracting (or adding) by a real number and then dividing (or multiplying) by a real number.

Example Solve $9x-2=-4$. By adding $2$ to both sides of the equation, $9x=-2$. By dividing both sides of the equation by $9$, $x=-\frac29$.
Example Solve $9x-2=-4$. By adding $2$ to both sides of the equation, $9x=-2$. By dividing both sides of the equation by $9$, $x=-\frac29$.

Next we look at a few examples which can be solvied by first dividing (or multiplying) by a real number and then subtracting (or adding) by a real number.

Example Solve $5(x+4)=25$. By dividing both sides of the equation by $5$, $x+4=5$. By subtracting $4$ from both sides of the equation, $x=1$.
Example Solve $\frac{x+8}{12}=3$ . By multiplying both sides of the equation by $12$, $x+8=36$. By subtracting $8$ from both sides of the equation, $x=28$.

Linear equations in one variable do not get much more sophisticated. Solving them is just a matter of applying a sequence of subtractions (or additions) and divisions (or multiplications) by real numbers.

Example Solve $\frac{\frac{5x-1}{4}+9}{2}=a$ . By multiplying both sides of the equation by $12$, $x+8=36$. By subtracting $8$ from both sides of the equation, $x=28$.
