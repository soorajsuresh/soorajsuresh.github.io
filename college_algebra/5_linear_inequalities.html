Linear Inequalities

Definition The relations $\le$, $\ge$, $<$, $>$, and $\ne$ are called inequality relations and are read less than or equal to, greater than or equal to, less than, greater than, and not equal to. The relations $<$, $>$, and $\ne$ are called strict inequality relations. When the equality relation $=$ in a linear equation is replaced by any inequality relation, the resulting relationship is called a linear inequality.
