If the equation

$ \mathbf{r}(u,v) = x(u,v)\mathbf{i} + y(u,v)\mathbf{j} + z(u,v)\mathbf{k} $
defines a continuous vector function on a region $R$ in the $uv$-plane that is one-to-one (exactly one input for every output and exactly one output for every input) on the interior of $R$, then the range of $\mathbf{r}$ (all outputs) is called the surface $S$ parameterized by $\mathbf{r}$. The defining equation of $\mathbf{r}$ along with its domain is called a parameterization of $S$. The variables $u$ and $v$ are called parameters, and $R$ is called the parameter domain.

Parametrize the cone $z = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$, $0 \le z \le 1$.
Parametrize the sphere $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = R^2$.
Parametrize the cylinder $x^2 + (y-3)^2 = 9$, $0 \le z \le 5$.

The partial derivatives of $\mathbf{r}$ with respect to $u$ and $v$ are

$ \mathbf{r}_u = \frac{\partial \mathbf{r}}{\partial u} = \frac{\partial x}{\partial u} \mathbf{i} + \frac{\partial y}{\partial u} \mathbf{j} + \frac{\partial z}{\partial u} \mathbf{k}, $
and
$ \mathbf{r}_v = \frac{\partial \mathbf{r}}{\partial v} = \frac{\partial x}{\partial v} \mathbf{i} + \frac{\partial y}{\partial v} \mathbf{j} + \frac{\partial z}{\partial v} \mathbf{k}. $

A surface parametrized by $\mathbf{r}(u,v)$, $(u,v) \in R$, is called smooth if $\mathbf{r}_u$ and $\mathbf{r}_v$ are both continuous and $\mathbf{r}_u \times \mathbf{r}_v \ne \mathbf{0}$ on the interior of $R$.

The area of the smooth surface $S$ parametrized by $\mathbf{r}(u,v) = x(u,v)\mathbf{i} + y(u,v)\mathbf{j} + z(u,v)\mathbf{k}$ over a region $R$ is

$ A(S) = \iint_R \left| \mathbf{r}_u \times \mathbf{r}_v \right| \, dA $
The differential $|\mathbf{r}_u \times \mathbf{r}_v|\, dA$ is called the surface area differential or surface area element.

Parametrize $z = f(x,y)$ over a region $R$ in the $xy$-plane and find the surface area element.
