Let $\F$ be a vector field defined on an open region $D$ in space and $A$ and $B$ points in $D$.

If $\int_C \F \cdot d\mathbf{r}$ has the same value for any path $C$ in $D$ from $A$ to $B$, then $\int_C \F \cdot d\mathbf{r}$ is called path independent in $D$, and $\F$ is called conservative on $D$.

If $\F = \nabla f$, then $f$ is called a potential function for $\F$.

An open region $D$ is called connected if any two points in $D$ can be joined by a smooth curve lying in $D$.

An open region $D$ is called simply connected if every loop in $D$ can be contracted to a point in $D$ without leaving $D$.

Neither connectivity nor simple connectivity implies the other. Think of connected regions as being in "one piece" and simply connected regions as having no "loop-catching holes".

Fundamental Theorem of Line Integrals
If $C$ is a smooth curve parameterized by $\mathbf{r}(t)$, $a \le t \le b$, and $f$ is a differentiable function in the plane or space whose gradient $\nabla f$ is continuous on a domain containing $C$, then
$ \int_C \nabla f \cdot \T \, ds = f(\mathbf{r}(b)) - f(\mathbf{r}(a)) $
A vector field $\F$ that is continuous over an open connected region in space is conservative if and only if it is a gradient field.
The vector field $\F$ is conservative on $D$ if and only if $\int_C \F \cdot \T \, ds = 0$ along every loop $C$ in $D$.

So, $\F = \nabla f$ on $D$ if and only if $\F$ is conservative on $D$ if and only if $\int_C \F \cdot \T \, ds = 0$ along every loop $C$ in $D$.

The vector field $\F(x,y,z) = P\mathbf{i} + Q\mathbf{j} + R\mathbf{k}$ on an open simply connected domain whose components have continuous first partial derivatives is conservative if and only if
$ R_y = Q_z, \quad P_z = R_x, \quad Q_x = P_y $

So, $\F = P\mathbf{i} + Q\mathbf{j} + R\mathbf{k}$ is conservative if and only if

$ (R_y - Q_z) \mathbf{i} + (P_z - R_x) \mathbf{j} + (Q_x - P_y) \mathbf{k} = \mathbf{0} $

The vector on the left is called the curl of $\F$ and is denoted $\mathrm{curl}\, \F$.

By abuse of notation,

\begin{aligned} \nabla \times \F &= \langle \partial_x, \partial_y, \partial_z \rangle \times \langle P, Q, R \rangle \\ &= \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ \partial_x & \partial_y & \partial_z \\ P & Q & R \end{vmatrix} \\ &= (\partial_y R - \partial_z Q)\mathbf{i} + (\partial_z P - \partial_x R)\mathbf{j} + (\partial_x Q - \partial_y P)\mathbf{k} \\ &= \mathrm{curl}\, \F \end{aligned}
