Extreme Values and Saddle Points

Let $f$ be a function of two variables $x$ and $y$ defined on a region $R$ containing the point $(a,b)$.

The value $f(a,b)$ is a local maximum of $f$ if $f(a,b)\ge f(x,y)$ for all $(x,y)$ in an open disk centered at $(a,b)$. It is an absolute maximum on $R$ if $f(a,b)\ge f(x,y)$ for all $(x,y)$ in $R$.

The value $f(a,b)$ is a local minimum of $f$ if $f(a,b)\le f(x,y)$ for all $(x,y)$ in an open disk centered at $(a,b)$. It is an absolute minimum on $R$ if $f(a,b)\le f(x,y)$ for all $(x,y)$ in $R$.

The plural form of maximum is maxima, and of minimum is minima. Both are called extreme values or extrema. The singular form is extremum. Local extrema are also called relative extrema.

If $f(x,y)$ has a local maximum or minimum at an interior point $(a,b)$ and the first partials exist there, then $f_x(a,b)=0$ and $f_y(a,b)=0$.
Describe the only points at which a function $f(x,y)$ can have local extrema.
If $(a,b)$ is an interior point of the domain of $f(x,y)$ such that either:
  1. $f_x(a,b)=0$ and $f_y(a,b)=0$, or
  2. one of $f_x(a,b)$ and $f_y(a,b)$ does not exist,
then $(a,b)$ is called a critical point of $f$.
A function $f(x,y)$ can have local extreme values only at critical points. Absolute extrema can occur at critical points and boundary points.

As in the single-variable case, a critical point may not yield a local extremum.

Let $(a,b)$ be a critical point of a differentiable function $f(x,y)$. If in every open disk centered at $(a,b)$ there exist points $(x,y)$ with $f(x,y)>f(a,b)$ and points $(x,y)$ with $f(x,y)saddle point of $f$.
The function $D$ defined by
$D(x,y) = f_{xx}(x,y) f_{yy}(x,y) - f_{xy}(x,y) f_{yx}(x,y)$
is called the discriminant or Hessian of $f$. It is the determinant of the Hessian matrix
$Hf = \begin{bmatrix} f_{xx} & f_{xy} \\ f_{yx} & f_{yy} \end{bmatrix}$.
If the second partials are continuous, then $D = f_{xx} f_{yy} - f_{xy}^2$.
Second Derivative Test for Local Extrema (SDT)
Let $f$ and its first and second partials be continuous in a disk centered at a critical point $(a,b)$, and let $D$ be the Hessian.
  1. If $D(a,b) > 0$:
    1. If $f_{xx}(a,b) < 0$, $f$ has a local maximum at $(a,b)$.
    2. If $f_{xx}(a,b) > 0$, $f$ has a local minimum at $(a,b)$.
  2. If $D(a,b) < 0$, $(a,b)$ is a saddle point of $f$.

Find local extrema and saddle points for $f(x,y) = x^2 - y^4$.
Find local extrema and saddle points for $f(x,y) = 3y^2 - 2y^3 - 3x^2 + 6xy$.
Find local extrema and saddle points for $f(x,y) = 10xy e^{-(x^2+y^2)}$.
