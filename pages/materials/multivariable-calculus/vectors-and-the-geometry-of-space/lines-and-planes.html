Determine equations for a line in space passing through a point $P_0=(x_0,y_0,z_0)$ parallel to a vector $\d=\inner{a,b,c}$.
Determine a vector equation and parametric equations for the line that passes through $(-1,3,0)$ and is parallel to $\d=\inner{4,1,-1}$.
Determine a vector equation and parametric equations for the line containing the points $P=(-2,1,-2)$ and $Q=(3,-3,1)$.
Parametrize the line segment joining $P=(-2,1,-2)$ and $Q=(3,-3,1)$.
Determine the distance from a point $S$ to a line that passes through a point $P_0$ parallel to a vector $\d$.
Determine the distance between the parallel lines $L_1$ and $L_2$ if $L_1$ is given by
$x=5-t$, $y=2-t$, $z=3t$, $t\in(-\infty,\infty)$,
and $L_2$ is given by
$ \r=\inner{0,4,1}+s\inner{1,1,-3}$, $s\in(-\infty,\infty)$.
Determine the point of intersection of the lines $L_1$ and $L_2$ and the (acute) angle between them if $L_1$ is given by
$\r_1=\inner{-4,9,-5}+t\inner{2,-1,0}$, $t\in(-\infty,\infty)$,
and $L_2$ is given by
$\r_2=\inner{0,4,1}+s\inner{1,1,-3}$, $s\in(-\infty,\infty)$.
Determine equations for a plane in space passing through a point $P_0=(x_0,y_0,z_0)$ normal to a nonzero vector $\n=\inner{a,b,c}$.
Determine an equation for the plane through $P_0(4,1,-1)$ with normal vector $\n=\inner{1,-7,2}$.
Determine an equation for the plane through $P_0=(2,0,2)$ perpendicular to the line with parametric equations $x=4+2t$, $y=t$, $z=-3-t$, $t\in(-\infty,\infty)$.
Determine an equation for the plane containing the lines given by
$\r_1=\inner{1,-1,4}+t\inner{1,-1,1}$, $\r_2=\inner{5,0,2}+s\inner{0,3,6}$, $t,s\in(-\infty,\infty)$.
Find an equation for the plane containing $P=(1,1,2)$, $Q=(0,0,-2)$, and $R=(0,-4,1)$.
Determine the line of intersection and dihedral angle between the planes $4x-2y+z=9$ and $2x+3y-3z=14$.
Determine the distance from a point $S$ to a plane through a point $P_0$ with normal vector $\n$.
Determine the distance between parallel planes with equations $ax+by+cz+d_1=0$ and $ax+by+cz+d_2=0$ (where $a$, $b$, and $c$ are not all $0$).
Find the point at which the line given by $\r=\inner{4,-1,2}+t\inner{3,3,5}$ intersects the plane given by $2x-2y+z+11=0$.
Determine if the following pair of lines intersect. If they intersect, present the point of intersection and the angle between them. If they do not intersect, then determine if they are parallel or skew, and present the distance between them.
$L_1:x=1+t,\quad y=2-5t,\quad z=6-t$
$L_2:x=3+s,\quad y=-8+s,\quad z=4+s$
Determine if the following pair of lines intersect. If they intersect, present the point of intersection and the angle between them. If they do not intersect, then determine if they are parallel or skew, and present the distance between them.
$L_1:x=1+2t,\quad y=2+8t,\quad z=3-3t$
$L_2:x=5+s,\quad y=-1+4s,\quad z=5-\frac32s$
Determine if the following pairs of lines intersect. If they intersect, present the point of intersection and the angle between them. If they do not intersect, then determine if they are parallel or skew, and present the distance between them.
$L_1=\build{(x,y,z)\in\R^3}{x=2+t,\quad y=4-t,\quad z=3+2t,\quad t\in(-\infty,\infty)}$
$L_2=\build{(x,y,z)\in\R^3}{x=2s,\quad y=-1+s,\quad z=3-2s,\quad s\in(-\infty,\infty)}$
