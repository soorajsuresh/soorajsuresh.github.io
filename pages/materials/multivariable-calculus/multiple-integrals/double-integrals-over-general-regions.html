Double Integrals over General Regions

Let $f$ be a function defined on a bounded region $R$ (which may not be a rectangle).

Cover $R$ with $n$ rectangles divided by a grid of lines parallel to the coordinate axes. The rectangles that lie completely in $R$ form a partition of $R$. These rectangles may not completely cover $R$ if the boundary of $R$ is curved.

Number the rectangles $1, \dots, n$, with areas $\Delta A_1, \dots, \Delta A_n$. Evaluate $f$ at a point $(x_k, y_k)$ in rectangle $k$ and multiply by its area. The sum:

$S_n = f(x_1,y_1)\Delta A_1 + \dots + f(x_n,y_n)\Delta A_n = \sum_{k=1}^n f(x_k,y_k)\Delta A_k$

is a Riemann sum of $f$ over $R$.

If the limit exists as $n \to \infty$ and is independent of the partition and points chosen, $f$ is integrable over $R$, and the limit is the double integral:

$\iint_R f(x,y) \, dA$

For positive $f$, the double integral represents the volume under the surface $z = f(x,y)$ over $R$.

If $f$ is continuous over $R$, a region bounded by $x = a$, $x = b$, $y = g_1(x)$, and $y = g_2(x)$, find a way to compute:
$\iint_R f(x,y) \, dA$
If $f$ is continuous over $R$, a region bounded by $x = h_1(y)$, $x = h_2(y)$, $y = c$, and $y = d$, find a way to compute:
$\iint_R f(x,y) \, dA$
Fubini's Theorem
Suppose $f$ is continuous on $R$.
\text{If } R = \{(x,y) \mid a \le x \le b, \ g_1(x) \le y \le g_2(x)\}, \quad \iint_R f(x,y)\,dA = \int_a^b \int_{g_1(x)}^{g_2(x)} f(x,y)\,dy\,dx
\text{If } R = \{(x,y) \mid h_1(y) \le x \le h_2(y), \ c \le y \le d\}, \quad \iint_R f(x,y)\,dA = \int_c^d \int_{h_1(y)}^{h_2(y)} f(x,y)\,dx\,dy
Properties of Double Integrals
Let $f$ and $g$ be continuous on a bounded region $R$. Then:
  1. $\iint_R c f(x,y) \, dA = c \iint_R f(x,y) \, dA$
  2. $\iint_R [f(x,y) \pm g(x,y)] \, dA = \iint_R f(x,y) \, dA \pm \iint_R g(x,y) \, dA$
  3. If $R = R_1 \cup R_2$ with $R_1 \cap R_2 = \emptyset$, then:
    $\iint_R f(x,y) \, dA = \iint_{R_1} f(x,y) \, dA + \iint_{R_2} f(x,y) \, dA$
Evaluate:
$\int_1^2 \int_0^{2x} x y^3 \, dy \, dx$
If $R$ is the region in the first quadrant bounded by $y = x$, $y = \sqrt{3} x$, and $x^2 + y^2 = 1$, find:
$\iint_R x y \, dA$
Evaluate:
$\int_0^{2\sqrt{\ln 3}} \int_{y/2}^{\sqrt{\ln 3}} e^{x^2} \, dx \, dy$
