Applications

If $\delta(x,y,z)$ is the density of an object occupying a solid region $D$ in space, then

$ \iiint_D \delta(x,y,z)\, \mathrm{d}V $
is called the mass of the object.

The first moment about each of the $xy$-, $xz$-, and $yz$- coordinate planes of an object occupying a solid region $D$ in space is

$M_{xy} = \iiint_D z \;\delta(x,y,z)\, \mathrm{d}V, \quad M_{xz} = \iiint_D y \;\delta(x,y,z)\, \mathrm{d}V, \text{ and } \quad M_{yz} = \iiint_D x \;\delta(x,y,z)\, \mathrm{d}V$

The center of mass is the point $(\bar x, \bar y, \bar z)$ where

$ \bar x = \frac{M_{yz}}{M}, \quad \bar y = \frac{M_{xz}}{M}, \quad \bar z = \frac{M_{xy}}{M} $

Analogous double integrals give the mass, first moments, and center of mass of two-dimensional objects (thin plates), which, for appropriate regions, are equivalent to the mass, first moments, and center of mass defined by single integrals in integral calculus.

If an object has constant density, then its center of mass is called the centroid of the object.

Present the centroid of the solid occupying the region under the surface $z=4-x^2-y^2$ above the region $R$ in the $xy$-plane given by $x^2+y^2 \le 4$.

The second moment or moment of inertia about each of the $x$-, $y$-, and $z$- axes of an object occupying a solid region $D$ in space is

$ I_x = \iiint_D (y^2 + z^2)\; \delta(x,y,z)\, \mathrm{d}V, \quad I_y = \iiint_D (x^2 + z^2)\; \delta(x,y,z)\, \mathrm{d}V, \text{ and } $
$ I_z = \iiint_D (x^2 + y^2)\; \delta(x,y,z)\, \mathrm{d}V $

Once again, analogous double integrals give the moments of inertia of two-dimensional objects (thin plates).

Present the second moments of inertia of a rectangular solid of constant density $\delta$ centered about the origin with lengths $a$, $b$, and $c$ parallel to the $x$-, $y$-, and $z$- axes.

The probability that the variables $X$ and $Y$ take on a value in a region $R$ in the plane is

$ P((X,Y) \in R) = \iint_R f(x,y)\, \mathrm{d}A $

If $f$ is nonnegative and $\iint_{\mathbb{R}^2} f(x,y)\, \mathrm{d}A = 1$, then $f$ is called a joint probability density function.

If $R$ has a finite area $A$ and $f(x,y) = \frac{1}{A}$ for all $(x,y) \in R$ and $f(x,y) = 0$ for all other $(x,y)$ in the plane, then the pair of variables $X$ and $Y$ is said to have a uniform distribution.

A random number generator uniformly generates two random real numbers $X$ and $Y$ in succession, the first chosen between $0$ and $10$, and the second chosen between $0$ and $5$. Present the joint probability function for $(X,Y)$ and the probability that $X$ is larger than $Y$.
