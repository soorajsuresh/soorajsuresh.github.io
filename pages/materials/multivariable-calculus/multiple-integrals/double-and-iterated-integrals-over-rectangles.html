Double and Iterated Integrals over Rectangles

If $A$ and $B$ are sets, then the Cartesian product $A \times B$ is the set of ordered pairs:
$A \times B = \{(a,b) \mid a \in A, \ b \in B\}$
If $A = [1,2]$ and $B = [3,5]$, give a geometric description of $A \times B$.

Let $f$ be a function defined on the rectangle $R = [a,b] \times [c,d]$.

Divide $R$ into $n$ smaller rectangles by lines parallel to the coordinate axes. These rectangles form a partition of $R$.

If a rectangle has width $\Delta x$ and height $\Delta y$, then its area is:

$\Delta A = \Delta x \Delta y$

Number the rectangles $1, \dots, n$, with areas $\Delta A_1, \dots, \Delta A_n$. Evaluate $f$ at a point $(x_k, y_k)$ in rectangle $k$ and multiply by its area. The sum:

$S_n = f(x_1,y_1)\Delta A_1 + \dots + f(x_n,y_n)\Delta A_n = \sum_{k=1}^n f(x_k,y_k)\Delta A_k$

is a Riemann sum of $f$ over $R$.

If the limit exists as $n \to \infty$ and is independent of the partition and points chosen, $f$ is integrable over $R$, and the limit is the double integral:

$\iint_R f(x,y) \, dA$

For positive $f$, the double integral represents the volume under the surface $z = f(x,y)$ over $R$.

Find $\iint_R (4 - x - y) \, dA$ over $R = [0,2] \times [0,1]$.

Double integrals can be written as iterated or repeated integrals.

Fubini's Theorem
If $f$ is continuous on $R = [a,b] \times [c,d]$, then
$\iint_R f(x,y) \, dA = \int_a^b \int_c^d f(x,y) \, dy \, dx = \int_c^d \int_a^b f(x,y) \, dx \, dy$

Double integrals of continuous functions can be computed as iterated integrals in either order using the FTC.

Evaluate using Fubini's Theorem in both orders:
$I = \int_0^\pi \int_0^2 x \sin(xy) \, dy \, dx$
Let $R = [a,b] \times [c,d]$ and $f(x,y) = g(x) h(y)$. If $g(x)$ and $h(y)$ are integrable over $[a,b]$ and $[c,d]$, then find:
$\iint_R g(x) h(y) \, dA$
Evaluate:
$I = \iint_D f(x,y) \, dA$, for $f(x,y) = e^y \cos x$, $D = [0, \pi/6] \times [0, \ln 3]$
If $R = [a,b] \times [c,d]$, find:
$I = \iint_R \frac{4x^2 y^2 - 7x^2 y + 5x^2}{(y^3 - 2y^2 + y)\sqrt{5 - 3x^2}} \, dA$
If $\Omega = [a,b] \times [c,d]$, find:
$I = \iint_\Omega \frac{e^{5x}(60y^5 + 150y^4 + 228y^3 + 231y^2 + 128y + 41)}{\sqrt{e^{10x} - 1} \ln^8(e^{5x} + \sqrt{e^{10x} - 1})(10y^3 + 15y^2 + 13y + 4)} \, dA$
