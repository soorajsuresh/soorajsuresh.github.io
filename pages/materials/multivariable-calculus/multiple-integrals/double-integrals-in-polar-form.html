Double Integrals in Polar Form

Let $f$ be a function of $r$ and $\theta$ defined over a region $R$ bounded by the rays $\theta=\alpha$ and $\theta=\beta$ and the continuous curves $r=g_1(\theta)$ and $r=g_2(\theta)$, where $0\le g_1(\theta)\le g_2(\theta)$ for $\alpha\le\theta\le\beta$.

Let $Q$ be the region described by $0\le r\le a$ and $\alpha\le\theta\le\beta$, where $a\ge g_2(\theta)$.

Cover $Q$ with rays $\theta=\alpha$, $\theta=\alpha+\Delta\theta$, ..., $\theta=\beta$, and circular arcs with radii $\Delta r$, $2\Delta r$, ..., $m\Delta r$, where $\Delta r = a/m$. The resulting patches are polar rectangles forming a partition of $Q$.

Number the polar rectangles inside $R$ as $\Delta A_1,\dots,\Delta A_n$, and let $(r_k,\theta_k)$ be a point in rectangle $k$. Taking $r_k$ as the average radius of the inner and outer arcs, the area of rectangle $k$ is:

$\Delta A_k = r_k \Delta r \Delta\theta$

The Riemann sum is:

$S_n = \sum_{k=1}^n f(r_k,\theta_k)\Delta A_k = \sum_{k=1}^n f(r_k,\theta_k) r_k \Delta r \Delta\theta$

If the limit exists as the partition is refined:

$\iint_R f(r,\theta) \, dA = \iint_R f(r,\theta) r \, dr \, d\theta$

In iterated form (Fubini's Theorem for polar coordinates):

$\iint_R f(r,\theta) \, dA = \int_{\theta=\alpha}^{\theta=\beta} \int_{r=g_1(\theta)}^{r=g_2(\theta)} f(r,\theta) r \, dr \, d\theta$
