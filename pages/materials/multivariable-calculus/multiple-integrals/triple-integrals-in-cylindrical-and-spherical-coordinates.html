Triple Integrals in Cylindrical and Spherical Coordinates

The point $(r,\theta,z)$ in cylindrical coordinates $r$, $\theta$, and $z$ represents a point $P=(x,y,z)$ in space for which $(r,\theta)$ are the polar coordinates of $(x,y)$.

Relate the rectangular (also known as cartesian) coordinates with cylindrical coordinates.
Graph $z=z_0$, $r=r_0$, and $\theta=\theta_0$.

Let $f$ be a function of $r$, $\theta$, and $z$ defined over a closed bounded solid region $D$. Contain $D$ in a solid right circular cylinder and divide it into $n$ cylindrical wedges by planes parallel to the $xy$-plane, right circular cylinders about the $z$-axis, and planes containing the $z$-axis.

For the cylindrical wedges lying inside $D$, number them $1$, $\dots$, $n$ and their corresponding volumes $\Delta V_1$, $\dots$, $\Delta V_n$, and let $(r_k,\theta_k,z_k)$ expressed in cylindrical coordinates be the point in the center of the $k$\textsuperscript{th} wedge.

Denote the change in the cylindrical coordinates $z$, $r$, and $\theta$ in the $k$\textsuperscript{th} cylindrical wedge by $\Delta z_k$, $\Delta r_k$, and $\Delta\theta_k$. From our development of double integrals in polar form, the area of the base of the wedge is $\Delta A_k = r_k \Delta r_k \Delta\theta_k$, so the volume of the wedge is $\Delta V_k = \Delta z_k \Delta A_k = r_k \Delta z_k \Delta r_k \Delta \theta_k$.

Evaluating $f$ at $(r_k,\theta_k,z_k)$, multiplying by $\Delta V_k$, and summing the products for each $k$ in $1$, $\dots$, $n$, we get the Riemann sum

$ S_n = \sum_{k=1}^n f(r_k,\theta_k,z_k) r_k \Delta z_k \Delta r_k \Delta \theta_k $

If the limit

$ \lim_{n \to \infty} S_n $
exists and has the same value regardless of the chosen partition and points in each wedge, then $f$ is integrable over $D$, and the limit is the triple integral of $f$ over $D$ in cylindrical coordinates
$ \iiint_D f(r,\theta,z) \, r \, \mathrm{d}z \, \mathrm{d}r \, \mathrm{d}\theta $
which is evaluated as iterated integrals
$ \iiint\limits_D f(r,\theta,z) r \, \mathrm{d}z \, \mathrm{d}r \, \mathrm{d}\theta = \int\limits_{\theta=\alpha}^{\theta=\beta} \int\limits_{r=h_1(\theta)}^{r=h_2(\theta)} \int\limits_{z=g_1(r,\theta)}^{z=g_2(r,\theta)} f(r \cos\theta, r \sin\theta, z) r \, \mathrm{d}z \, \mathrm{d}r \, \mathrm{d}\theta $

$ \iiint_D r \, \mathrm{d}z \, \mathrm{d}r \, \mathrm{d}\theta $

The point $(\rho,\phi,\theta)$ in spherical coordinates $\rho$, $\phi$, and $\theta$ represents a point $P=(x,y,z)$ such that $P$ is a distance $\rho$ from the origin, $\phi$ is the angle in $[0,\pi]$ that $OP$ makes with the positive $z$-axis, and $\theta$ is the angle from the cylindrical coordinates $(r,\theta,z)$ of $P$.

Relate rectangular, cylindrical, and spherical coordinates.
Graph $\rho=\rho_0$, $\phi=\phi_0$, and $\theta=\theta_0$.

Let $f$ be a function of $\rho$, $\phi$, and $\theta$ defined over a closed bounded solid region $D$. Contain $D$ in a solid sphere and divide it into $n$ spherical wedges by spheres centered at the origin, cones through the origin, and planes containing the $z$-axis.

For the spherical wedges lying inside $D$, number them $1$, $\dots$, $n$ and their corresponding volumes $\Delta V_1$, $\dots$, $\Delta V_n$.

Denote the change in the spherical coordinates $\rho$, $\phi$, and $\theta$ in the $k$\textsuperscript{th} wedge by $\Delta \rho_k$, $\Delta\phi_k$, and $\Delta\theta_k$. The circular arcs for which $\phi$ changes are of length $\rho_k \Delta\phi_k$. The circular arcs for which $\theta$ changes are of length $\rho_k \sin\phi_k \Delta\theta_k$. The remaining edges are line segments of length $\Delta \rho_k$. A point $(\rho_k,\phi_k,\theta_k)$ can be chosen in the $k$\textsuperscript{th} wedge such that the volume of the wedge is $\Delta V_k = \rho_k^2 \sin\phi_k \Delta\rho_k \Delta\phi_k \Delta\theta_k$.

Evaluating $f$ at $(\rho_k,\phi_k,\theta_k)$, multiplying by $\Delta V_k$, and summing for each $k$ in $1$, $\dots$, $n$, we get the Riemann sum

$ S_n = \sum_{k=1}^n f(\rho_k,\phi_k,\theta_k) \rho_k^2 \sin\phi_k \Delta \rho_k \Delta \phi_k \Delta \theta_k $

If the limit

$ \lim_{n \to \infty} S_n $
exists and is independent of partition and points chosen, then $f$ is integrable over $D$, and the limit is the triple integral of $f$ over $D$ in spherical coordinates
$ \iiint_D f(\rho,\phi,\theta) \rho^2 \sin\phi \, \mathrm{d}\rho \, \mathrm{d}\phi \, \mathrm{d}\theta $
evaluated as iterated integrals
$ \iiint\limits_D f(\rho,\phi,\theta) \rho^2 \sin\phi \, \mathrm{d}\rho \, \mathrm{d}\phi \, \mathrm{d}\theta = \int\limits_{\theta=\alpha}^{\theta=\beta} \int\limits_{\phi=h_1(\theta)}^{\phi=h_2(\theta)} \int\limits_{\rho=g_1(\phi,\theta)}^{\rho=g_2(\phi,\theta)} f(\rho,\phi,\theta) \rho^2 \sin\phi \, \mathrm{d}\rho \, \mathrm{d}\phi \, \mathrm{d}\theta $
