Triple Integrals in Rectangular Coordinates

Let $f(x,y,z)$ be defined on a closed bounded solid region $D$.

Contain $D$ in a rectangular box and divide the box into rectangular cells with planes parallel to the coordinate planes. Number the cells $1,\dots,n$ that lie completely inside $D$, with volumes $\Delta V_1,\dots,\Delta V_n$, where $\Delta V_k = \Delta x_k \Delta y_k \Delta z_k$.

For each $k$, evaluate $f$ at a point $(x_k,y_k,z_k)$ in cell $k$ and multiply by the cell's volume. Summing these products gives a Riemann sum:

S_n = \sum_{k=1}^n f(x_k,y_k,z_k)\Delta V_k

If the limit exists as the partition is refined:

\iiint_D f(x,y,z) \, dV = \lim_{n\to\infty} \sum_{k=1}^n f(x_k,y_k,z_k)\Delta V_k

In this case, $f$ is called integrable over $D$, and the limit is called the triple integral of $f$ over $D$.

Interpret the triple integral:
\iiint_D dV

A three-dimensional version of Fubini's Theorem allows evaluating a triple integral as an iterated integral. For example, if

D = \{ (x,y,z) \mid g_1(x,y)\le z\le g_2(x,y),\ h_1(x)\le y\le h_2(x),\ a\le x\le b \},

then

\iiint_D f(x,y,z) \, dV = \int_a^b \int_{h_1(x)}^{h_2(x)} \int_{g_1(x,y)}^{g_2(x,y)} f(x,y,z) \, dz \, dy \, dx

The properties of triple integrals are analogous to double integrals. Continuous functions are integrable over closed bounded solid regions with boundaries formed of finitely many smooth surfaces joined along finitely many smooth curves.
